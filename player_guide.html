Character Sheet Player Guide

Core Rules

Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency vs. DC. DCs: 10 easy, 13 moderate, 16 hard, 19 very hard, 22 nearly impossible.

Advantage/Disadvantage: Roll 2d20, take higher/lower.

Abilities

  • Strength (STR): Physical power, lifting, melee damage.
  • Dexterity (DEX): Agility, stealth, ranged accuracy.
  • Constitution (CON): Endurance, resilience, hit points.
  • Intelligence (INT): Knowledge, investigation, spellcasting (Wizards).
  • Wisdom (WIS): Perception, insight, survival, spellcasting (Clerics/Druids).
  • Charisma (CHA): Persuasion, performance, spellcasting (Bards/Sorcerers/Warlocks).

Common Items & Gear

  • Weapons: Longsword (1d8 slashing), Shortbow (1d6 piercing), Dagger (1d4 piercing), Greatsword (2d6 slashing)
  • Armor: Leather Armor (AC 11 + DEX), Chain Mail (AC 16, STR 13), Plate Armor (AC 18, STR 15)
  • Shield: +2 AC
  • Consumables: Health Potion (heals 2d4+2 HP), Antitoxin (removes poison), Alchemist's Fire (1d4 fire/round)
  • Tools & Gear: Backpack, Rope (50 ft), Crowbar, Torch, Healer's Kit

Basic Spells

  • Fireball (Evocation, L3): Explosion of flame at range.
  • Cure Wounds (Evocation, L1): Heals target for 1d8 + modifier.
  • Mage Armor (Abjuration, L1): Magical force grants AC protection.
  • Magic Missile (Evocation, L1): Creates three darts of magical force.
  • Shield (Abjuration, L1): Invisible barrier boosts AC.
  • Bless (Enchantment, L1): Boosts allies' rolls.
  • Detect Magic (Divination, L1): Sense magic nearby.
  • Healing Word (Evocation, L1): Heals target at range.
  • Thunderwave (Evocation, L1): Thunderous force sweeps out.
  • Sleep (Enchantment, L1): Puts creatures to sleep.
  • Burning Hands (Evocation, L1): Sheet of flame from hands.

Combat

Roll initiative (d20 + AGI). On your turn: move + action (+ bonus action). One reaction per round.

Resting

Short Rest: 1 hr; spend hit dice to heal. Long Rest: 8 hr; restore HP/resources.

Leveling

Proficiency Bonus +2 at L1; increases at 5, 9, 13.

Resolving Buffs and Debuffs

When a character is affected by both a buff and a debuff that apply to the same skill, spell, or action, use the following rules:

  • Advantage & Disadvantage: If you have both, they cancel each other out. If you have multiple sources of advantage and only one source of disadvantage (or vice versa), you still roll only one d20 (no stacking).
  • Stacking Bonuses/Penalties: If you have multiple buffs or debuffs that grant a bonus or penalty to the same roll (e.g., +1d4 and +1d6), add all bonuses together before rolling. The same applies to penalties.
  • Ongoing Effects: If you have both a buff and a debuff that cause ongoing effects (like healing and damage each round), apply both each round (e.g., heal 1d4 and take 1d4 damage).
  • GM Adjudication: If there is any doubt or a unique situation, the GM has the final say on how effects interact.

How Long Do Buffs and Debuffs Last?

Most buffs and debuffs are sustained (continue each round) until removed by a spell, ability, or successful saving throw. At the end of each affected character's turn, they may attempt to end a sustained effect by rolling a saving throw (usually d20 + relevant modifier) against a DC set by the GM or the effect's source. If the roll meets or exceeds the DC, the effect ends. Otherwise, it continues.

  • Example: If you are Burning (taking 1d4 fire damage per round), at the end of your turn you might roll a Dexterity or Constitution saving throw (GM's choice) against DC 13. If you succeed, the burning ends; if not, it continues next round.
  • Some effects have a fixed duration (e.g., "lasts 1 minute" or "3 rounds") or end automatically when a condition is met (e.g., healed, submerged in water, etc.).
  • Ask your GM for the specific save type and DC for each effect.

The GM may adjust how effects are sustained or ended to fit the story or encounter.

If you are unsure how to resolve a combination of effects, ask your GM for clarification.

How Status Effects Interact with Skills & Spells

Some status effects directly buff or debuff specific skills and spells. For example, Inspired can buff skills like Performance, Persuasion, or spells like Fireball and Magic Missile. Poisoned can debuff Stealth, Medicine, and healing spells.
